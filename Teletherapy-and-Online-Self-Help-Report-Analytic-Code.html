Teletherapy and Online Self-Help - Updated

Study Overview

This study investigates interest in teletherapy and online self-guided mental health programs in the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic among undergraduate students at a large university. The goal of this study is to gather evidence on the extent of demand for these services during the COVID-19 pandemic, whether it differs by individual characteristics, and whether it differs by the characteristics of the services offered. We believe that the findings from this study can inform public health decisions regarding the role of teletherapy and online self-help in an effective COVID-19 response. Specifically, we seek to answer the following questions:

  1. What is the extent of interest in teletherapy and online self-help during the COVID-19 pandemic among members of a large university community?
  2. Does interest in teletherapy and online self-help differ by (a) sociodemographic factors and/or (b) levels of depression and anxiety symptomatology?
  3. Does interest in teletherapy and online self-help vary as a function of cost (i.e.free vs $30/session)?

This is an exploratory study and we do not have specific hypotheses regarding the extent of interest in teletherapy and online self-help, or whether/how interest differs across the above conditions.

Analyses are limited to complete cases only. This drops 174 of 1403 cases.
